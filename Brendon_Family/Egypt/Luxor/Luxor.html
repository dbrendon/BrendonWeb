Luxor

January 6th, 7th, 8th - Mercure Coralia

  • Luxor Temple; avenue of sphinxes, obelisk, pylons, statues
  • Karnak Temple; more and more and more - we're fans of hypostyle halls
  • horse drawn carriages; main mode of transporting tourists
  • ferry; the way to get across the Nile, cheap and you're with the locals
  • Corniche; similar to Aswan's but not as pretty, at least it allowed one to be a bit removed from the taxis and carriages however there was always the Felucca dealers
  • Egyptian circus; tried to go but we were a day early, were allowed to enter the circus tent with one ring, don't think we missed much
  • Valley of the Kings; took the local ferry across the Nile to the West Bank, we bought one 3 tomb ticket each so we saw Ramses III, Taworsret/Sethnakht and Tuthmosis III, we didn't go inside King Tut's tomb (he was a lesser Pharaoh so the tomb itself is nothing much and we saw the treasures at the Egyptian Museum in Cairo) but we did sing Bob Friesen's song "Tut is there room in your tomb for me?, I want to live to eternity,...." outside of it, climbed over the mountain from the Valley of the Kings to Deir al-Bahri (Temple of Hatchepsut), checked out Medinat Habu (Temple of Ramses III)
  • Tourist Police; everywhere, up on platforms along the Corniche and at each corner, more so here than Aswan (the 1997 massacre took place in Luxor)
  • "imshi" meaning "go away" reserved for children; didn't work very well on the children here
