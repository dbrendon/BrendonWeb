Aswan

January 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th - New Cataract Hotel

  • breathtaking view from our hotel room of Nile and feluccas, Elephantine Island, contrast of desert mountains to the lushness surrounding the Nile, Old Cataract Hotel
  • Abu Simbel; abrupt start to the day when 3:15 am wakeup call never happened and phone rang at 4:15 to say our van was outside hotel and the convoy leaves at 4:30 am, 3 hour minibus ride just north of the Sudan border, impressive but looses a bit of authenticity as it was moved from original site and is very touristy, at least we can say "been there, done that"
  • Tourist Police/Police; everywhere, up on platforms along the Corniche and at each corner
  • Old and New Cataract Hotel; these two hotels share the grounds/pool/gardens, the nice things about staying at the New Cataract is that it's not as costly as the Old Cataract and you don't have to look at it, we were able to visit the Old Cataract's enchanting lobby and deck (Agatha Christie's Death on the Nile) which other tourists not staying at the New or Old are unable to enter, Kerala spent most of her time swimming, gorgeous view
  • Felucca; rush of the water at the cataract, peacefulness when on west side of Elephantine island, we took beer and peanuts along, according to the popular phrase we heard continually during our travels we "chilled out"
  • Corniche; lovely walk way along the Nile
  • Small World; as I was lounging around the pool I admired this lovely woman with a British accent playing in the pool with her daughters and how she interacted with her mother-in-law (an assumption I made which was correct) and how she smiled, a bit later Kerala and I walked by this woman and I don't know why but I told her of how I enjoyed watching her play with her daughters and that she looked so good, when Kerala and I walked back past this woman she thanked me for the compliment and asked where I was from. I said "Canada", she said "Where abouts?", I said "between Niagara Falls and Toronto", she said "Where abouts?" - now this totally threw me - she said "I'm from Burlington" - I told her "I'm from Stoney Creek" and then continued to go on about how familiar I am with Burlington having David and I both worked there and family and friends living there and somewhere in the spiel mentioned that we were living in Denmark for a year and then she asked me, are you ready?, "Do you know the Polks?" - we both went giddy - she's Gord and Margot's neighbour and one of their daughters does synchronized swimming with Shannon - we couldn't believe it - apparently on Christmas Eve the neighbours got together for a drink and upon hearing that she and her family were heading to Egypt Gord and Margot told them about us. Imagine, and we weren't even supposed to have been in Aswan that day as we had cancelled the overnight felucca ride.
  • Aswan Moon; a restaurant on a barge on the Nile off the Corniche, we went for dinner and drinks each evening, two gentlemen befriended Kerala while she was dancing on the gang plank and that was the beginning of a wonderful friendship, music and dancing, song with her name "lay a lay a Kerala leena lay a", Kerala was given a Nubian nickname 'Noora', magic tricks, party for Kerala (dancing on top of a floating table - check out the website), English Engineers on leave from the Dam thought it was Kerala's birthday and gave her chocolate bars
  • Nubian Museum; visited this beautiful museum documenting the Nubian way of life before the Aswan Dam was constructed and flooded their ancestral homeland, surrounded by a lovely water garden
  • Nubian Village; Kerala's friend from Aswan Moon, Mahamed Orabi, invited us to his village and a camel ride, we couldn't make it in the morning so he said that we were welcome to come in the afternoon but there was a Greek tour group coming through so we wouldn't be alone, we met at Aswan Moon and he took us over by ferry across the Nile to his village, Kerala held a baby crocodile in the market, we had tea at his Aunt's home while his cousin painted henna (Nubian designs) on Kerala, when the Greek tour group came into the courtyard we felt like we were on display, Orabi then led Kerala outside the courtyard to see a sea of camels (46 in total - 43 Greeks and the three of us), Kerala was asked to chose which camel (Mona Liza) she wished to ride, she could have gone on her own but we thought it was safer for Daddy to go with her, Mommy followed on her own camel (Owen). The most exciting part of the ride is when you mount and dismount as you must move your weight with the camel as they get up or kneel. After riding across the desert we got on a felucca to go back to our hotel. We met Orabi that evening at Aswan Moon and he wouldn't allow us to even buy him a drink as "you are sitting at my table".
