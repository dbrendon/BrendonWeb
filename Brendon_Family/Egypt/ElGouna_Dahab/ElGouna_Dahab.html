El Gouna





El Gouna

January 9th, 10th - Sheraton Miramar www.elgouna.com

  • getting there; took a bus from Luxor to Hurghada (5 hours) and then climbed onto a mini-bus that we were told headed to El Gouna, after a few blocks all the people got off and the driver had to get a translator, he took us one more block and then we got on a mini-bus which had other tourists who were heading for El Gouna, we reached El Gouna "downtown" and then had to buy tickets for the mini-bus that would take us to the hotel (tickets were 10 pounds each for the week - relatively costly to other taxi fares and we were only here 2 days)
  • first impressions; David was ready to leave immediately as the town was so contrived however after going into town that night having a half descent meal at a descent price and able to get groceries and the next day enjoying the pool he was a happy camper and would suggest this as a destination spot
  • architecture; "pastel-painted post-modernism creation designed by internationally renowned architect Michael Graves looks resplendent in it seaside desert setting" - Lonely Planet "ditto" - The Brendons, do you think Nubian style would work on Lakeview Drive?
  • mountains of Sinai across the water
  • turquoise and multi blue/green coloured sea
  • pools; several pools heated, playing Marineland, Marco Polo and "pretending" with Kerala
  • steam bath and sauna
  • town; fully self-contained resort town built by one of Egypt's biggest tycoons, non-tourists are required to show id upon entering the town's gates, shuttle service vans between Marina, Town Centre and major hotels, real estate offices to purchase homes built to your design taste starting at $176,000 US, similar to Seaside or Carillon in the Florida pan handle
  • low number of tourists; the Sheraton usually has a minimum of 500 to 700 - at the time we were there - only 200

    Dahab

    January 12th, 13th, 14th, 15th - Hilton Dahab

  • snorkeling Red Sea; multi-coloured coral reefs/walls and fish just steps from shore and/or accessible via short boat trips, Kerala waving to a scuba diver below, a multitude of fish of various colours, shapes and sizes including a barracuda, buoyancy of the salt water
  • windsurfing; David braved the inconsistent wind of overpowering gusts, 5% completion on jibes but was not alone
  • check points; although we encountered them throughout Egypt they were more frequent in the Sinai peninsula, bus was stopped and passports requested (once before going into tunnel under Suez canal and then again after coming out of the tunnel)
  • St. Katherine's Monastery; burning bush (rather green not at all burnt up), elaborate decorations in Church (amazing how the ornaments and gold were brought in)
  • climbing Mount Sinai; it took us 2.5 hours of climbing using the relatively shallow incline of the camel trail, it's only now that David and Brenda share their thoughts of turning back at the hour mark but were encouraged to continue due to Kerala's stamina, Kerala had us pretending to be following Moses up to the top (Daddy walked, Mommy rode a donkey (difficult holding your hand as though you're holding the harness of the donkey all the way up), Kerala herding sheep with her stick), disappointment when reached the summit as view was spectacular but obstructed by the many tea/snack shacks. On the return trip took the alternate path - the 3750 Steps of Repentance (laid by a monk) down, although faster it was harder on the legs
  • dramatic sunsets; the swirls of pink and purple and greater defined mountain layers was spectacular especially since it was happy hour
  • happy hours; two for one 5 to 6 pm at the Coconut Bar and then 6 to 7 pm at the Windjammer Bar, we became regulars and created an entourage, nightcap at the Bedouin tent on the beach
  • beach; since the pool wasn't heated we found ourselves on the lounge chairs at the beach, Kerala was popular with all the fellows who worked at the various kiosks, horse ride
  • views of Saudi Arabia across the Gulf of Aqaba

    • January 16th - bus from Dahab to Cairo

    Flight "Home":

    January 18/19 :- took taxi to Cairo airport for around midnight, the major street/highway from Cairo to the airport was closed off and both sides were lined with 100s, no must have been 1000s, of soldiers for President Mubarak's entourage to drive in from airport we arrived at the airport, managed to have our flight from Amsterdam to Copenhagen moved up to around 11:00 am/noon instead of 3:15 pm, attempted to sleep in airport, guard/soldier expecting baksheesh on my return from the washroom (had to pay the lady who handed you toilet paper) as he had told me, without me asking, where the washroom was when I was on the way to it

    Flight KL554 Cairo 3:55 to Amsterdam 7:55 - watched a fantastic sunrise at 9:00 am at the Amsterdam airport while Kerala played in the kids' area and David slept felt good to brush teeth with tap water

    Flight Amsterdam (around noon) to Copenhagen (around 2:00) - after scrambling after hearing last minute announcement that the train was arriving on another track it felt like we were "home", train station is around the corner from our apartment, good to return home to the apartment well cared for by neighbour Britt

    We had thought we'd wait until Kerala was older to do this type of travelling with her as we wanted to make sure she could hold her own and remember it. Well she definitely proved that she, in her words, can "standle" it and hopefully with the combination of photos, video and this documentation she'll be aided in remembering it. Now to decide on another exotic destination while Kerala is at this remarkable age perhaps Thailand, Central and/or South America or Kerala.

    Ma'as salama,
    Love, David, Brenda & Kerala
