Cairo

December 25th, 26th, 27th - Sheraton El Gizera
December 28th, 29th - Mayfair
January 17th - Sheraton El Gizera

  • sounds; honking, "welcome to Cairo" (has there been a tourism campaign?), street vendors, call to prayer, music cranked up beyond 11, beeping of metal detectors
  • traffic; dodging taxis/cars/buses/service vans/bicycles/horse drawn carts and carriages, people in general, people carrying loads on their heads or pushing carts, goats/sheep
  • rain in a desert city; puddle hopping, pant legs sprayed with mud up to our knees
  • social order; appalling way in which Egyptians with money conduct themselves, how generous those without are
  • shop groupings; streets of weighing scales, cloth, paper products, musical instruments, car parts, trim and fringe, eyeglasses, spices, door handles, shoes - scrambling of illegal vendors when the police came down the road - blood drenched floor of the meat market with heads of cows on display
  • Pyramids; Saqqara and Giza pyramids including the Sphinx, climbed through narrow passage way to the chamber inside the Pyramid of Khafre (Chephren), magnificence of the pyramids was truly appreciated when seen from afar relative to modern skyscrapers, Pizza Hut has the best view (music turned off during Call to Prayer)
  • Sound and Light Show at Pyramids; had to do it (because James Bond did it) - even though it rained it was okay
  • Egyptian Museum; how did they get those statues inside?, Mummy room - no nightmares where Kerala's concerned, being a bit more lenient on the Vatican Museum for hoarding the Egyptian artifacts as there's oodles to spread around
  • Coptic Cairo; January 7 "Merry Coptic Christmas", Hanging Church, Church of St Sergius (Kerala received special blessing) & St Barbara and Ben Ezra Synagogue
  • City of the Dead; friendly greeting from the people who live in this cemetery, climbed to top of the Mosque of Qaitbey's minaret past all the wires and cables for the sound system (a picture of this mosque is on the Egyptian one pound note)
  • Khan Al-Khalili Souq; a must visit for the alleys and history but bad experience when doing business, we weren't sure whether we were out of whack with respect to the prices but they seemed exorbitant and not even worth starting to haggle as the price was absurdly high. After a while we'd just walk away without even negotiating. Our suspicions and concerns were confirmed with a few instances: - a shopkeeper proclaimed that he'd give us a good price because of "Dear Kerala" - as we were looking at a belly dancing hat a tourist rushing to get on her tour bus selected a similar hat and I listened to the negotiating and final price when it was our turn the exact same lowest price was given to us - later at a spice stall we were quoted 45 Egyptian pounds for 100 grams of Frankenscence just outside the market we paid 2 for the same amount and Kerala was given a pack of Chiclets, boutiques in major hotels provided a lovely atmosphere to shop and fixed prices which were comparable to the market however the selection wasn't as great. Needless to say we did not return with our souvenir wish list completely checked off. Perhaps Pier I or Indisk will have similar goods at comparable prices. Our advise to future visitors is don't rely on buying all your souvenirs in Cairo, check out the hotel boutiques and ensure your wonderful Egyptian experience is not tainted by this market.
  • subway; the way to travel around, efficient, clean and cheap, front two cars of each train are reserved for women and children only, warm reception from fellow travelers
  • train station; woman go to the front of the line when buying tickets, Wagonslit sleeper train cost too much and maximum two people in cubicle so we'd have to book two cubicles
  • women going from shop to shop swinging a smoking pot of frankenscence for good luck
