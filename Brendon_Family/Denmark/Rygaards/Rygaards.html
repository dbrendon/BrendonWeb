Denmark - Rygaards School

First Day at Rygaards School


All I want for Christmas ...

Kerala seems to always have a story behind how she loses her teeth. David received a phone call at work to hear "Mr. Brendon your daughter Kerala had an accident with a door" and crying in the background. Kerala was put on the phone and David spoke to her and calmed her down - I believe there was promise of ice-cream in the conversation. What happened was... There are very heavy solid fire doors throughout the school (an old manor house) which the kids must leverage themselves against and pull hard to open. Kerala's class was heading through one of these doors to go outside for the school's 92nd birthday celebration and someone on the other side pushed it open and it banged her in the mouth. She lost her two top front baby teeth which were ready to come out any day and received a bit of a fat lip. Apparently there was a lot of blood but the two teeth were recovered. David called me immediately and I ran to the school to find Kerala watching the birthday celebrations from the main foyer window. She told me that the "Church Girls" tried to be nice but they only speak Danish. A couple of nuns came by and consoled Kerala in Danish. When they left Kerala told me that they were the "Church Girls" she told me about. She came home with me and seemed in fine spirits. She doesn't know when she'll put the teeth under her pillow for the tooth fairy. She has to prepare a note describing what happened. Later that day she went to her street dance (hip hop)class. We met "pop man" at the store (he owns the pizza shop around the corner and always gives Kerala a bottle of pop - he can hardly speak English). She showed him her toothless grin and told him how it happened. He gave her 10kr ($2) so she bought some chocolate dairy products. We can't believe how big the gap is. She has requested lots of "ogurt"(yoghurt) to eat. It's fun to listen to her speak.
