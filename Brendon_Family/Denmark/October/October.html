October Update

October 4th

We rented a car "weekend special", picked Kerala up at noon on the Friday (October 4th) and headed across the island of Zealand to the island of Funen ($50 to cross the bridge).

We first attempted to see Nyborg Slot, a castle, since we thought we were going right by it. We followed a few signs but then gave up as we really wished to spend our time doing the labyrinth around Egeskov Castle. Unfortunately Egeskov was closed for the season - we should have taken the Lonely Planet guide of Scandinavia Lis and Dan gave us instead of the older one specific to Denmark we borrowed from the library as it was outdated.

We made our way to Faaborg which was a bustling harbour town in the 17th century. The three of us took a quick walking tour. Kerala liked the mock Viking ship. There was a very questionable sculpture which involved two cows and a man. Funen's landscape was littered with thatched roof homes - people living in them, not museum pieces. The rolling green hills, brilliant blue sky with white puffy clouds had us thinking we were in TeleTubby land. We even saw a whole rainbow.

We then drove off Funen to Jutland which is not an island but a peninsula which is attached to Europe. We were fortunate to come across lodging as everything was booked. The hotel lowered the price to be within our budget - we paid "only" $150 Cdn for a standard Best Western room (Kerala stayed free) - can't imagine what the prices or availability would be like during the high season as this is the off season. We were disappointed as the pool the hotel has access to was not open.

The next day we headed up to Skagen (a town of yellow-washed buildings) to the most northern point of Denmark,Grenen, to see the waves from the two seas clashing together. We headed across the sand dunes in the driving rain/wind to the point. On our way we searched for amber which may have washed up but had no luck. We had a very interesting hike to Tilsandede Kirke (Buried Church) which has been buried under the sand. The drive up Jutland was very nice - rolling hills - reminded me a bit of Wisconsin. Instead of "punch bug" we played "punch windmill". These are the modern windmills; slim tall column with 3 blades. They're everywhere and look so majestic and eerie. We managed again to find a spot to stay for the night (a bit of sweating it).

The next day we went to Legoland (Billund, where Lego was created). We felt we got our monies worth in the first few minutes that Kerala was in the park. She's at a perfect age as she could go on all the rides (except traffic school as you have to be 8 years old) because she is tall enough for the roller coasters yet short enough for the kiddie rides. Her favourite rides are the rollercoasters. The three of us really enjoyed the Extreme Racers which had you take pretty much a vertical drop and then you were sprayed with mist at the bottom. We watched a 3D movie based on Extreme Racers. We wore 3D glasses and there were real affects such as cool wind, rain, streamers. Not only was Kerala at the perfect age with respect to the rides but she also appreciated how everything was made of Lego. However she was sad that she could not "shrink" so that she could live in the little lego villages. If it weren't for the $100 bridge charges we'd go to Legoland just for the day (I believe there is some rebate you can get at Legoland for the bridge if you just go for the day). We drove home that night. There were a few towns that I would have liked to have seen but we didn't have the time: Ribe (oldest town in Denmark), Esbjerg (Mennesket ved Havet - neat landmark) and of course Egeskov Castle. Perhaps we'll go when we're on our way to other parts of Europe or when visitors come who wish to go to Legoland.

October 8th

We had a visit from Jordan Robertson! He was in Europe on a business trip and flew in from Paris to visit a Copenhagen client. He brought provisions including insulating film for the windows. We needed to see a familiar face - it felt good.

Kerala and I met him at the airport and then we drove back into Copenhagen in his rental car. Of course we visited the Little Mermaid and Jordan actually visited her on her rock - very brave and he didn't get a soaker.

We went by Amalienborg Palace where the guards were on night patrol. We then met up with David at Nyhavn where Jordan enjoyed seeing the Chris-Craft boat which was moored there (Diane and Jordan have one which we spent a weekend enjoying up near Orillia). We went for dinner in Nyhavn and caught up with each other and how Diane and the boys are doing.

October 17th - Sweden Trip

On our travels, Kerala is able to stay free at the hotels as she "shares our bed". However we bring a thermarest and sleeping bag. We can't imagine her actually sleeping with us - a "double" bed are really two twin beds pushed together therefore there's a gap or frame down the middle. Thank goodness Kerala is really good about sleeping on the floor. At all the hotels we stayed in Denmark, Portugal and Sweden breakfast buffet is included. The main items were bread, cheeses (circular slicer), coldcuts, coffee and juice. There is usually also scrambled and soft boiled eggs (fantastic quiche at the Lisbon hotel), cereals (not sure about the "milk" for the cereal - sweet cream?) and sometimes sausage or bacon.

October 26th

We celebrated my birthday (6 days after the fact) - thank you for the cards, gifts and phone calls. We took it easy at the start of the day. David went to the bakery and selected a new bread for us to try (that's his mission every Saturday morning). He also picked up a special pastry for me (as close to pain au chocolat as he could get). I cracked open a bottle of Irish Cream, David had picked up at the duty free on a ferry from Germany to Denmark. In the afternoon we went on my traditional birthday hike. We hiked around Charlottelund (forested area with trails, thatched roof buildings and a former palace/manor house) the sun appeared a few times, which was a real treat as it made the leaves a lovely golden colour (not much red happening here). We walked down to the beach and came across this huge dock with small green buildings with white trim out on the water. It said it was open to the public in the summer 40 kr/adult and in the winter you needed to be a member. We then noticed a man showering nude inside and people walking along the dock holding towels and using a key to go in. We asked a woman what it was and she said that people went swimming in the sea. There were no saunas simply lockers where you could put your clothes and go swimming without sand getting in your hair. People go swimming here all year round and there's an 8 year waiting list for membership - go figure. We then took the train into Copenhagen where we went to the Jazz Bar for a beer and music. We always seem to meet very interesting people at our usual "international" table. We then walked along the Stroget. Enjoyed some of the busking musicians along the way. We came across an Indian restaurant that had a buffet. There was no child's menu so Kerala had the pizza and salad buffet but kept eyeing our selection. Copenhagen keeps growing on me each time I go "downtown".

October 29th

We were dreading it and it happened this morning - one hour fall back. It has been already getting dark too early. Here's what we get to look forward to: November 1 sunrise 7:15 am, sunset 4:31 pm; December 1 sunrise 8:23 am, sunset 3:37 pm; January 1 sunrise 8:40 am sunset 3:48 pm; February 1 8:08 am sunset 4:39 pm. (see Sunrise/Sunset Comparison) We've been fortunate with the weather so far. It's been uncommonly dry and warm however this past month it has cooled down and has been more rainy. The bike shop fellow quoted some statistics that the bicycle association put together. Apparently for a regular cyclist over the course of the year they will encounter rain 10 times. David is up to at least 6 so far!!!

I'm enrolled in a Danish course. The course starts November 12 and is Tuesday to Friday, 9:00 am to 12:55 pm, until December 20. It's been a lot of running around, cutting through red tape, muddling through the bureaucracy, dealing with the selective interpretation of English, imcompetent people resulting in many "Connie Schmidt" moments (we've coined this after an experience David's colleague had dealing with a travel agent) - I can't imagine someone who is introverted and English is not their first language - they wouldn't stand a chance. The end result for me is that my Kommune is subsidizing me to go out of Kommune for my lessons. I was told that the funding is 8000 kr per course, that's $1600. After I picked up my book at StudieSkolen I walked to where the class will be held. I thought the directions were pretty neat - go past the palace. On my way I went by two mounted police and walked along with a procession of palace guards on their return from the changing of the guards.

This Tuesday we're going to our Kommune's open house. Hopefully we'll find out what our taxes are paying for, other than my Danish course, and some of the recreational offerings. Beer will be served!!!

There's Hallowe'en costumes and decorations on sale everywhere here however we just found out some disappointing news; they don't 'trick or treat' here and there is no dress-up at Kerala's school. Apparently all the Hallowe'en items are for private parties. In lieu we're holding a Devil's Eve Party on Wednesday after school. Kerala made and sent out pumpkin invitations to some friends. I'm not too sure how I'm going to get the pumpkin to the apartment. The challenge is carrying it up to the 4th floor. Yes the 4th floor they can't fool me by calling this the 3rd floor.

Last Thursday was UN Day so Kerala's school had a special celebration with a UN lunch. The amount and selection of food was remarkable. You name a country there was food from it. As a Canadian with minimal resources ie. no caribou or maple syrup that didn't cost an arm and a leg I improvised. Popcorn balls with syrup and peanut butter cookies. I actually used a cup of our peanut butter stash. They were a hit. It was very odd to be able to bring them to such an event. People look at you oddly when you refer to peanut allergies. Some Moms were dressed up in traditional outfits; Chinese, Indian and Rawandanese and the Canadian with her Team Canada t-shirt. I was actually included in photos of the group of Moms who had dressed up.

On Friday I was to go with Kerala's class to Rosenburg Castle as part of their study of buildings but unfortunately the class trip was cancelled as the other Year 2 teacher was sick.

Kerala is still enjoying everything. We're going to make a list of castles she has visited. She's always pretending. She does have her moments of missing other kids but thankfully they're rare. She's been a lot of fun to travel with, nothing phases her. She keeps up with us walking and that's usually the whole time. She's also developed a sense of humour (just as warped as her parents) and we now have a lot of inside jokes. One of her front teeth is starting to grow in and her bottom right permanent molar is starting to come in. I went into Toys 'R Us a month ago and right at the entrance was a Barbie airplane which is amongst all the toys stacked to the ceiling of her toy room which she left back in Canada. Tears came to my eyes as I thought of how she has not mentioned missing any of it. We're very fortunate. She enjoys school and seems to be learning a lot. She likes going through odd and even numbers and counting by 10s. She also likes to write. She's very fond of the question mark.

David is gearing up for a major project with work. He'll be away in Holland (close to Germany near Dusseldorf) for most of November and into December even away for over a weekend. He bikes to work each day it's a 45 minute ride. He's gotten to a point where he refuses to take the train even on rainy days. We're looking at buying him a new used bike as the one that came with the apartment isn't that great.

We're considering a weekend trip to Barcelona in a few weekends. There's a flight promotion for the month of November being offered, but with David away we think it might be a bit much. We've even thought that we might have to have Kerala and I meet him at the airport for him to fly in and fly out again. We'll see.

As for Christmas, if I'm not going home I need to do something special. Of course my eyes are welling up with tears as I write this as we won't be home with you all and it'll be the first time I won't wake up Christmas morning at 9 Avalon Avenue. David is feeling very much the same about this. He even thought of heading to India - he's after somewhere warm and different. We've tentatively booked flights to Egypt. The flight leaves for Cairo on the 25th of December (KLM, change planes in Amsterdam) and we return on January 19th. We still have to do some research with respect to required shots, weather, ... Our decision needs to be made by Friday. Other destinations are booked and even if available are very expensive.

We're looking at doing a lot of home made paper decorations for Christmas. We understand that Christmas is very special here in Copenhagen - like a Norman Rockwell painting. There are special beers that are available during the season and special pastries (I now know what the odd frying pan with the dimples is for) are traditional. They actually put candles on Christmas trees. We plan to take Kerala to the Nutcracker since she won't be home for Nana and Papa to take her. Apparently, Christmas Eve is when the Danes celebrate. Christmas Day is not the main focus. We're considering inviting the people who live below us and across from us in the apartment for an evening just before Christmas. We brought over a few Christmas CDs and piano carol books. I wonder if anyone will show and/or partake - should be interesting.

Things are more expensive here then we were led to believe. On average it's over 100% more. For example a pair of running shoes for Kerala runs at least 300 kr which when converted to Cdn dollars is $60 - for a kids running shoe not Nike or anything special. We happened across a 10 and 20 kr store which is very much like our "Buck or Two" stores however it costs $2 or $4.

Everyone keeps asking how my parents and sister are doing. I should have a status report on our website. I'm glad to say that everyone is well. Although Diane finds the scar area a bit sensitive she feels back to her usual energetic self which means working mornings as a Speech Language Pathologist with the Hamilton Catholic School Board, volunteering in Evan and Tosh's classes Monday and Friday afternoons and being co-chair with her sister-in-law Esther of the Parkside School Council. Dad goes to the Cardiac Care Exercise Program at the General Hospital. After his first day he claimed "they're trying to kill us". Mom is down to once a month to the transplant clinic and twice a week for blood testing. They continue to keep an eye on the swelling (water or blood) that's on the new kidney.

Well I better go help David locate the other end of this vent that is in our apartment which has gusts of cold air coming down it. We're bracing ourselves for a long, cold, dark winter. Our approach is to take advantage of what a large city has to offer and also to travel.
