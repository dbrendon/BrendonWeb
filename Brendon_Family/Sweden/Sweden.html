Eskilstuna

After one day at home Kerala and I went with David on a business trip to Sweden. We got up really early and took the train to Helsingor and then the ferry over to Sweden. We rented a car then drove 7 hours to the Stockholm airport. The drive was very pretty. Very much like our "up north". There were many red wood houses with white trim and oh yes, many McDonalds. We did see an IKEA but we didn't go into one. We picked up Mike Popovic at the international airport after finding out that the airport we first went to which is closest to Stockholm only does domestic. The four of us drove a couple of hours to Eskiltuna. We stayed in a hotel which used to be the first VOLVO factory. David and Mike went out to dinner with the clients. Kerala and I went to McDonalds. When David got back he went for a hottub with the some of the people he had been out to dinner with. Kerala and I discovered that there was a free buffet dinner so we had a second supper. The fellow who worked the desk gave Kerala a bag of chips - for the young lady.

The next day while David and Mike were working Kerala and I had a sauna and a hot tub. When in Sweden...... Kerala was reluctant to pull the chain to let the sauna steam. It took a lot of convincing her that Mommy wouldn't get her in trouble. Kerala and I then went to a factory museum which was really neat. There was factory machinery from the turn of the century an environment I have imagined Frederick Taylor doing time and motion studies in. There was an old fire engine, a steam engine and a lithograph machine just like the one my Mom and Dad had at the Print Shop when I was a kid. Late afternoon after the meeting was completed the four of us drove to Stockholm where we'd stay for the rest of the weekend. Mike treated us to dinner at the Hard Rock Cafe.

Stockholm

The next day the four of us went sightseeing. We couldn't help but notice that the Swedes have something against pigeons. On top of every sign, light, rod there were beds of spikes/needles preventing the birds from resting on top. However we sympathized with the Swedes as the few structures that were not covered by these spikes were covered in bird dirt. Another fascination was the way in which the leaves were dropping off the trees. None of us had ever seen leaves drop off a tree at that volume. We headed over to old Stockholm, Gamla Stan. We saw the changing of the guards at the palace. We really enjoyed the marching band. They did some modern pieces as well and the head soldier initiated the crowd to clap along. The band had very fancy footwork. Gloomy memories of when our high school concert band attempted to be a marching band. It was very cold. So we warmed up with 2 hot chocolates and 1 coffee which cost $20 Cdn. We couldn't find any Mats Sundin souvenirs for Evan and Tosh. We didn't see any ABBA memorabilia either but then I believe there's an exhibit at the museum. We said our good-byes to Mike who was going to visit the Nobel museum (he would later say it wasn't worth it - lots of reading). Kerala, David and I then took a ferry to Djurgarden. We went to Junibacken an adventure land based around the stories written by Astrid Lindgren. We waited a long time to go on a ride (all inside) which is similar to the Peter Pan one in Disney World and saw scenes from different stories this person has written. When the ride finished we came out to just outside Pippi Longstockings house and there was Pippi sitting on horse. Pippi didn't stay long though as she was being chased by the police and jumped onto the ride we had just come off. We thought Pippi would return but she didn't. Of course I complained as we paid about $75 Cdn for the three of us so at least we should have more Pippi. Without much resistance the Manager gave me a FULL refund. On our way back to the hotel we walked along the water. This part of Stockholm was more what I had expected. We made our way along the pedestrian walkway. We didn't feel up to a sit down dinner so we found a shwarma place (very popular in Scandinavia - lots of Turks) then went back to our dinky little hotel room.

The next day we went on a three hour boat tour aboard the ship named Stockholm around Stockholm's Achipelago. It's like going on a boat ride up north except it's not Canadian Shield it's Baltic Shield. Many of the cottages have saunas at the waters edge. It was a lovely day and very relaxing. It took us up to Vaxholm where we circled around Vaxholm castle and made our way back. We then drove back to Helsingor. On the way home, about the middle of Sweden, we came across snow, lost of snow and it was only the 20th of October (my birthday). Kerala and I had a snow ball fight. We took the ferry back to Denmark and then took the train home.
