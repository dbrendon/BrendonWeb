Portugal Part 1: Lisbon

Kerala and I accompanied David on this business trip.Ā We left Wednesday (October 9th) for Portugal.Ā We took the train to the Copenhagen Airport and flew Air Portugal.Ā We were escorted by Barnaby Bear, Kerala's class bear who has a passport and a suitcase.Ā Kerala missed only 1.5 days as there is an autumn holiday the week of the 14th and the 11th was a half day.ĀĀ We rented a car and drove into Lisbon.Ā David was amazing at the wheel managing the 5 lane round about with 7 or 8 spokes.Ā Our hotel was very "fancy schmacy" (nicest we've ever been in) sort of a nouveau art deco influence and a pool on the roof top.Ā

While David worked on Thursday Kerala and I explored Lisbon.Ā I never would have thought that I'd be exploring Lisbon's back medieval alley ways with my 6 year old - what a great experience for both of us, oh yes and of course Barnaby.Ā Right away I loved Lisbon, sort of like how I felt about Istanbul (warmth of people, exotic/different enough,...).Ā There were shoeshiners, laminators, the smoke of the chestnut roasters (we tried them - blah), trams, laundry hanging everywhere, people peering out the half door/windows, dog poop in the middle of the sidewalk, cats, black cloaked initiators, cockerels everywhere, monuments/fountains, tiled ("Mommy's favourite") buildings/fountains, rolling motion sidewalks, buskers, peddlers, ... Kerala and I headed through Baixa down Rua Augusta checking out Praca Dom Pedro IV (Rossio), the funicular and then to Praca do Comercio.Ā We then went in hunt for the Fado museum and possible bars which had Fado (Portuguese blues) - a little Nellie Fertato (not sure how to spell her name). ĀKerala and I then checked out the docks and some of the old ships moored there and then the train station.Ā All the while there was a light rain.Ā We then went through Alfama which was a maze of alley ways.Ā What fun we had.Ā We checked out a few churches along the way - pretty impressive.Ā We had hoped to find the castle which we could see from the bottom of Baixa and Alfama but ended up overshooting it and found ourselves on the hill next to it.Ā Even now while looking at a map of Lisbon I have no idea where that was - I think it was Graca.Ā We had lunch while enjoying the beautiful view of the castle on the other hill and all of Lisbon.Ā We then made our way down the hill.Ā That was an adventure in itself which we would take Daddy on the next day as it was so "crazy" - narrow street with a substantial slope, numerous doorways to little shops, lots of people (not necessarily Portuguese), scaffolding, lots of traffic, different smells.Ā

We then went to see an archeological dig (Nucleo Arqueologico) which I had made an appointment to see for free at 5:00pm.Ā The ruins are believed to be the remnants of a Roman spa dating from 1st century AD.Ā Kerala and I then made our way back to the hotel.Ā

David made a long day of it in order to finish up his work that day.Ā That evening we headed down to the pedestrian area and checked out the many restaurants which all seemed to have the same menus (British flag indicating English translation).Ā Most restaurants have aquariums out front with a selection of seafood.Ā We decided on a restaurant which was very busy and did not have the British flag on the menu - this ended up being fun in itself.Ā We went with the recommendation of the waiter with respect to type of fish and then David had to select the actual fish from a box in the middle of the restaurant which has a clear lid.Ā It tasted lovely, however we're still not sure what type of fish it was (didn't understand the pronunciation and couldn't read the handwriting).Ā

The next day Kerala and David attempted to go for a swim but it started to rain and it was too cold.Ā Kerala and I then showed David around Lisbon.ĀĀ We first made our way up the walkways to the top of the hill in the Bairro Alto area in search of the cork store.Ā Lovely views from up there.Ā We made our way down checking out some shops on the way.Ā We went by the City Hall, through Praca do Comercio.Ā Kerala and I were quite excited to have Daddy experience Alfama.Ā The day before when Kerala and I were exploring I kept telling Kerala how much Daddy would like this - "Mommy was right". We allowed ourselves to get lost in the alleyways.Ā We kept seeing the castle but couldn't figure out which right or left, up or down, tunnel or stairs,... to take.Ā

We finally did find the castle, Castelo de Sao Jorge.Ā We had a late lunch there and then we climbed all over the walls of the castle.Ā You definitely would not want to be afraid of heights.Ā We then made our way down that "crazy" street.Ā It seemed to be more alive and was reminiscent of Chandi Chowk in Delhi, but western style.Ā Kerala played in the modern fountains of Praca da Figueira (at least that's where I think we were - somewhere just under the castle).Ā It's maddening how the maps don't have any topographical indicators.Ā We took David down to Rossio to check out the rolling motion square.Ā As described in Lonely Planet book, "undulating mosaic pattern of the cobbled pavement made of hard-cut white limestone and grey basalt cubes".Ā It's really cool.Ā We did one more time round Baixa and bought some souvenirs from a shop where women who were all dressed in uniform served you.

On Saturday we headed up the coast.Ā But first we wanted to have a good look at the Aqueduto das Aguas Livres (Aqueduct of Free Waters).Ā In our attempt to get a good view of it we ended up getting on to the highway which took us over a huge bridge.Ā As we crossed the river/strait, we had a close-up look at the huge Jesus statue - which from a distance only looks like a giant cross (very Rio de Jenario -like).. Unfortunately, we were so horrified about crossing this huge bridge by mistake that we turned back immediately. All the time thinking about the Denmark bridge (Zealand-Funan) which costs CAD$50 each way. We were ready to beg the toll bridge person to have mercy on us, but we needn't have worried as the toll was only 1 Euro ($1 US) and the toll was only for one way.Ā

We went to the top of Padrao dos Descobimentos (Discoveries Monument).Ā It's amazing what one will remember from Grade 6 Social Studies when trying to explain to a 6 year old who each statue is and what they accompished.Ā However, we saved the scurvy topic for her future teachers.Ā The size of the statues was quite daunting.Ā

We then drove to the Torre de Belem which is a castle that looks like a giant chesspiece.Ā We had fun walking around the various levels - up many flights of stairs and checking out all the turrets in this place.Ā All the time we had to pretend that Kerala was a Princess - or was it a Queen or was I the Queen and David the King or Prince - I couldn't keep up with which character I was supposed to be.Ā At one time Kerala had us pretending that she was a clam fisherman!?!Ā

We were fortunate that we were not bothered by the arrumadores (arrangers) but we did see them in action.Ā These are men that will stand in the middle of the road and wave people into public parking spaces and ask for a tip.Ā Once the space is occupied, they just move to the next open space.Ā Apparently it's not necessary to give them money - but if you wish your car to stay safe you might want to.Ā Not any different than an experience I had in aĀ municipal parking lot in the north end of Hamilton - perhaps those kids were Portuguese?
