Portugal Part 2: Sintra

We made our way out to Cascais, but unfortunately the weather wasn't that great so we didn't use hotel pool.Ā We went on a mission to find the fish market which was supposed to occur at the end of the day, but weren't able to locate it. However, we did watch the last fisherman of the day bring in his catch.Ā The docks were piled with fishing traps and rope.Ā Several dogs lay asleep atop of the piles of rope.Ā We walked around the quaint village - gorgeous.Ā The rolling motion sidewalks were a happenin'.Ā Many seaside restaurants.Ā The weather cleared a little and we were able to enjoy the sunset.Ā Lots of fishing.Ā We had an "okay" dinner in an out of the way place.Ā We finally got the hang of sending back the couvert - the cheeses, olives, bread, butter and other tidbits they put on your table and charge for even if you don't eat them.

The next day we headed up to Sintra.Ā David's "Need for Speed" became fulfilled as he gunned it up and down twisting alley ways and winding roads with a cliff on one side and a rock face on the other.Ā Luckily for me, but much to David's dismay the upgrade they gave us ended up being an automatic.Ā I can't imagine the ride if he'd had a standard.Ā So much for my cautious, defensive driver - who ever said that David drove like an old man - not the case anymore.Ā I kept thinking the side view mirror was going to break off due to how narrow some of the alleys were.Ā Kerala thought it was the greatest - "like a rollercoaster".Ā

Sintra was quite special.Ā We visited the palace in town called Palacio Nacional de Sintra. Afterwards we took a walk through the back alleys, not frequented by tourists, and stopped at a tiled fountain to take a photo.Ā Another couple came up and we took photos for each other.Ā The woman then looked at me and said "You're Canadian aren't you?".Ā I replied "Yes".Ā She then asked "Are you from Stoney Creek?"Ā Too much!Ā We tried to figure out where we knew each other from.Ā She was now working for Nortel in Paris.Ā (David accused her of owing him money - those share prices hurt).Ā The more we spoke the more familiar she seemed, but I couldn't place her.Ā She lived up on Green Hill off King Street.Ā We went through our high school sports history, but our teams would have never have played each other as she was with the Hamilton Catholic School board and I with Wentworth Public School Board.Ā As well she was at least 5 years younger than I.Ā She asked if I was a teacher.Ā I said "an instructor at McMaster" but that wasn't the connection.Ā She hadn't even gone to Mac.Ā Then David suggested perhaps through work.Ā I asked her about Bonar and that's where it all came together.Ā She was an Auditor with Price Waterhouse that I worked with.Ā All of a sudden I remembered all sorts of things about her and I think started scarying her with how much detail I recalled.Ā I still can't believe she recognized me.Ā Mystery solved.Ā It certainly is a small world.Ā Here we have two Stoney Creek gals, one living in Paris the other in Copenhagen, bumping into each other in a back alley of Portugal.Ā

We then headed up the mountain that rises above Sintra. We drove up as far as we could and then parked the car. Then we walked up even further to another palace, Palacio Nacional da Pena.Ā Kerala really liked this one.Ā It was very different with lots of odd shapes, colours, turrets, archways and huge rocks - almost Disney like.Ā

Next, we then made our way over to the top of the next hill/mountain to visit the castle, Castelo dos Mouros.Ā The forest we walked through to get to the castle was more like a jungle.Ā Supposedly this area is like a rain-forest micro-clime. We had some trouble getting as we didn't have the right ticket. We thought we had purchased a ticket for all sites, but apparently not. Unfortunately the ticket office was closed and the entrance was in the process of closing. After pleading with the gatekeeper about the ticket mix up (another couple had the same problem but they simply turned away) he let us in.ĀĀ Inside it was like the Great Wall of China.Ā We walked all along the walls.Ā It was the end of the day so there weren't too many other people.Ā

We then went back down to Sintra and went to an Azulejos (painted tiles) atelier.Ā We hastily purchased a set of tiles.Ā We choose a musical theme - the ever popular man playing a Fado guitar singing to a woman.Ā For dinner we thought we should try bacalhau (faithful friend) which is salted cod.Ā Egads!Ā Neither David or I could eat it.Ā The woman wasn't happy with us for not eating it - and she let us know it.Ā We headed back to our hotel but since it was dark we went the more direct flatter highway route.Ā There was a jazz trio playing in the hotel lounge, so the three of us went down to listen. Kerala thought it was very special to be able to stay up late and listen to jazz.

We spent a good portion of the next morning on a never ending highway.Ā We had hoped to take the fast way to Sintra and then back track along the coast (opposite of what we did the day before).Ā Instead we got on this highway that only had entrance ramps - and tolls!Ā Designed to take traffic straight into Lisbon from all the suburbs. We finally found our way back to Sintra, didn't stop, but instead continued on to Colares.Ā Once again David managed the driving through the narrow alleyways very well.Ā Finally a day of good weather.Ā We managed to do some beach hopping.Ā We had a picnic lunch at Paria das Macas.Ā We had fun fighting the flow of a stream that led to the ocean and was influenced by the waves.Ā The rocky area looked as though we had landed on the moon.Ā Kerala and David played their usual ocean game of "Run away from the waves".Ā They made a new one up which has one of them making a trail in the sand which the other has to follow.Ā It's funny watching them walking around in circles.Ā We saw quite a few surfers.Ā We guess it's just like us windsurfers, a lot of waiting, we wait for the right wind while in the case of surfers the right wave.Ā We went to the most western point of Europe, Cabo da Roca, and yelled out "Happy Thanksgiving" across the ocean to all of you in Canada.Ā Back at Cascais, we checked out the Boca do Inferno (Mouth of Hell) which ended up being just outside our hotel -Ā it was awesome.Ā We then went to a mall and had turkey stew for dinner.Ā We went to Jumbo which is like a Walmart, where we bought a flannel sheet set (which we hadn't been able to find in Denmark) and a housecoat for David.

Of course not knowing the Portugese language had it's moments.Ā There were some instances where our French and Spanish lessons paid off.Ā You could kind of guess what the words meant.Ā At one point I even had a conversation in French with a Portuguese woman - oh boy did my "head hurt" after that one.Ā David said he was impressed - in this case he was easily impressed.Ā It's very hard to be in a country which seafood is their specialty and you're not a seafood type of guy or gal.Ā We found people very friendly in Portugal.Ā There were a number of instances in which they would go out of their way to help.Ā People gave Kerala little gifts ie. chocolates, a tiny cockerel.Ā We were quite taken with Portugal for many reasons: the behaviour of the people, the warmer climate, greater selection of merchandise and cheaper.Ā We started to dream about having been placed in Lisbon rather than Copenhagen.

Over the course of our Portugal trip we took photos of Barnaby; among the cockerels, on the beach, climbing a castle,...Ā Kerala wasn't too keen on me putting Barnaby on a ledge to take a photo as she feared the wind would blow him over the cliff.Ā Barnaby even received a luggage tag and had his passport stamped at the hotel.Ā I e-mailed Kerala's teacher some of the photos which her class looked at.
