Twenty-five Years of My Life

"Time's fun when you're having flies," said one frog to the other.

Isn't it amazing how time does seem to fly the older we get; but has it all
been fun? Not in my case. Shortly after I left Green Acres in 1981 my wife,
Laura, had a stroke and together with the doctors we decided that a heart
valve replacement operation would improve her life. The operation went awry
and my entire life was turned upside down. My last teaching years at
Bellmoore School in Binbrook were difficult ones and I was happy for
retirement. I remarried four years after Laura's death, sold what I had
hoped to be my retirement home on the Chippawa Creek, purchased a
one-and-three-quarter acre lot near Fonthill and have lived there for
sixteen years. My life revolves around my family (my wife, three married
children and four grand kids), my hobbies (gardening and ham radio) and
church. Within my first year of retirement I had a heart attack with cardiac
arrest. This was followed seven years later with a quintuple bypass surgery.

In all of this I have found that life is too short to hold grudges and
resentments. To live life to the fullest one has to give of oneself for
others; happiness and contentment will be sure to follow.

"If you give, you will get! Your gift will return to you in full and
overflowing measure, pressed down, shaken together to make room for more,
and running over. Whatever measure you use to give - large or small - will
be used to measure what is given back to you." Taken from the Bible, the
Gospel of Luke.
